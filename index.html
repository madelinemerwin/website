Bio

Madeline Merwin is an American-born composer specializing in contemporary classical music composition and multimedia music projects. A current graduate student in Composition at the University of Michigan, Madeline received her Bachelor of Music degree in Composition at Vanderbilt Universityâ€™s Blair School of Music in 2021 and graduated from Interlochen Arts Academy with a major in Composition in 2017. Madeline is the winner of the 2018-19 American Prize in Composition, multimedia student division, for her dance/film/electroacoustic musical work "Ceilings Made of Glass". Madeline has received prestigious commissions from organizations such as the Nashville Ballet, Wright State University, and X-Contemporary Dance Ensemble, and enjoys working with classical and contemporary dance groups.

She also has experience in writing for film, and has attended the Filmscoring Academy of Europe Summer Program, Elsewhere she wrote and recorded multiple full orchestral short film scores. Madeline has written and directed numerous experimental music videos, and enjoys the collaborative nature of multimedia projects. In addition to her performance work, Madeline is passionate about activating for accessibility of classical music for modern audiences. She was selected as a TEDtalk presenter in 2018, and completed a TEDtalk on the socioeconomic barriers facing the classical music industry today. Madeline completed a thesis dissertation on the political motivations behind the delineation of opera and musical theatre as genres, and plans to pursue her interest in researching socioeconomic pressures in the music industry alongside her performance endeavors. In her free time, Madeline enjoys listening to TrueCrime podcasts and spending way too much money on candles.
